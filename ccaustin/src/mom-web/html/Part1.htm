TITLE:  Building Momentum through Biblical Giving

TEXT:  2 Corinthians 8:1-15                

ORIGINALLY TAUGHT:  April 2001

ORIGNINAL TITLE:  What the Bible says about giving?       Part 1

 

 

Our giving reflects our relationship with the Lord.

 

This message will answer these questions:

  1. Why should I give?
  2. When should I give?
  3. Where should I give?
  4. What happens when I give?
  5. How much should I give?
  6. Is there a right way and a wrong way to give?

 

Principles of Giving

 

Principle 1 (vs. 1-2) We are to give whether we have little or a lot.

  • They gave out of deep poverty.
  • They gave out of an appreciation of Gods grace (unmerited favor toward us)
    • Has God blessed you, redeemed you, and provided for you?
  • There is never a better time to give.  Its always difficult to start.  It always takes sacrifice and faith.  Dont give God your leftovers.
    • 2 Sam 24:24 David said that he wouldnt give to God something that cost him nothing.

 

 

Principle 2 (vs. 3-4) We are to give because we want to, not because we have to.

  • They gave according to their ability and beyond their ability.
    • Giving according to your ability often referred to as a tithe  (proportional giving  in proportion to their income, i.e. 10% of gross income).
    • Giving beyond your ability often referred to as offerings (sacrificial giving  over and above their tithe).
  • They were freely willing
    • Verse 4 they urged him to allow them to give to the Lord
    • Pressure tactics to make people give is wrong
      • We dont give to get, or give out of guilt, but in response to the grace of God, which not only opens our hearts but also our hands.
    • Giving because I want to, not because I have to

 

Principle 3 (vs. 5) Give as Jesus gave.

  • They gave more than Paul could have ever hoped for.
    • Looks like: first giving yourself to the Lord, and second to the ministry.
    • Giving doesnt start with a tithe check, but giving yourself first to God.
      • First you need to have a personal relationship with the Lord
      • You cannot buy your way into Heaven
      • Once you have given yourself, its easy to give the rest
    • Its an ownership issue.
      • Am I an owner of my resources or a steward of Gods resources
      • I am bought with the blood of Jesus and He now owns me and everything I have
      • Stewardship is managing someone elses resources (i.e. Gods)
    • Paul encourages them to be diligent in this area of Christian living (giving), just like they may be in other areas of their Christian living (devotionals, service, fellowship, evangelism, etc.) in verse 6-7.
    • Verse 8  Pauls says that it is not a command to give!
      • Our giving is not in response to the Law, but in response to love.  It is our worship of the Lord for His love toward us.
      • The Macedonians modeled giving and in Matt 6:1-4, Jesus said:
        • Dont draw attention to yourself
        • Dont be motivated by being acknowledged by others
          • But we can be encouraged \motivated by others in this area (from their modeling)
      • Pastor Allen doesnt know who  gives what or how much, and will never know this information.
        • Therefore youll never be thanked, nor will you be treated differently because of your giving level.
  • The Macedonian model is good, but the best is the Messiah model.
    • Verse 9 Jesus became poor so that we might become rich.
    • Philippians 2:5  We should have the same attitude as Christ Jesus, who although He was God, humbled Himself and died on the cross.  That is sacrificial giving!
    • When we look at Jesus gift on the cross we are motivated to give sacrificially, not out of guilt but in response to grace.

 

 

Principle 4 (vs. 10) Give based upon what we have.

  • Gods ways are often opposite of mans, in that He says that we will do better if we give first.
  • God asks us to do what we can, not what we cant.
    • The problem is that so many so-called Christians are not doing anything in the way of giving, not that they are not doing enough.
    • Most people say theyd give more if they made more.  Barna research disputes this notion, showing that the more someone makes, the less they are likely to give generously. 
      • 8% of those making $20K or less use tithe as a standard
      • 5% of those between $20-40K
      • 4% of those between $40-60K
      • 2% of those between $60-75K
      • 1% of those between $75-100K
      • 5% of those greater than $100K
      • According to Barna, the 1999 average giving for an adult in a church our size was $1500.  If they used a tenth as a standard, then their salaries would have been $15K.  For comparison, the per capita income in Austin for 1997 was $25K.
    • Theres a difference between a hand gift and heart gift.  It doesnt matter what was given, but what the heart was like in the gift.  If you gave a $100 but had a bad attitude, God sees the attitude and credits that amount.

 

Principle 5 (vs. 13) Give trusting God to meet your needs.

  • Exodus 16:18 the miracle of the manna is used to demonstrate Gods provision.
    • Gather what you need, share, but dont hoard.
  • In Haggai 1:6 the people never had enough.  It felt like they were putting their money in a bag with holes.
    • They had simply forgotten to put God first, and were putting themselves first.
  • Philippians 4:19 God promises to supply all our needs in Christ Jesus.
    • The context is that of Paul thanking the church for their generous gift.  The promise was to those putting God first and others before themselves.
  • He doesnt promise to meet our greeds, but our needs as we are giving.  When we do, we enable and free God to move in miraculous ways in our lives.

 

Many think that the Bible says, Money is the root of all evil, but it doesnt.  1 Timothy 6:10 states, the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil.  So its not money, but the love of money that is the problem.  Jesus clearly states that you cannot love both God and mammon (money).  Jesus and Money cannot occupy the same throne in our life. One of the ways that God breaks our bondage to money is through giving.  It keeps our focus on the Lord.
