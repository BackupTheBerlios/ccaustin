TITLE:  Building Momentum through Biblical Giving

TEXT:  2 Corinthians 8:16-9:15     

ORIGINALLY TAUGHT:  April 2001         

ORIGNINAL TITLE:  What the Bible says about giving?       Part 2

 

 

Allen reviews a few disclaimers concerning his teaching on the topic of giving:

  • He has never taught an entire message on the topic of money
  • We as a church have covered the Bible from cover to cover, going verse by verse, so we only teach about giving as often and in as much detail as the Bible does
  • It has been over 5 years since giving was even a main point of one of his messages

The purpose of this review was to point out that Allen and Calvary Chapel are not known for trying to manipulate people to give by teaching on money, but that as the Bible deals with a topic and we approach that section, we want to faithfully proclaim God’s Word and not shy back from teaching a potentially volatile topic.

 

These chapters in 2 Corinthians deal with the topic of giving.  In Part 1, we dealt with the Principles of Giving, and this week we will deal with the Practice of Giving.

 

Review

Paul was on his 3rd missionary journey, and while on this journey, one of the things he was doing was taking up an offering for Jewish Christians that were suffering in Israel.  He was sending 3 men to pick up the offering that had already been received.  The thrust of what Paul is saying in the end of chapter 8 is seen in verses 20 and 21.  He was saying that when it came to the way his ministry handled money, he wanted everything to be above board (without reproach). 

 

This is our desire as well at Calvary Austin.  To do this:

·         Allen does not handle the money

o       He does not count the money or deposit the money

·         Money is handled by 2 or more money handlers so that no one is alone with the money or counts it alone

·         Calvary expense checks require 2 signatures

·         Our Board consists of members inside and outside the church, and this Board approves the annual budget, reviews financial reports, and provides accountability and objectivity

 

Vs19 – says “the gift was administered to us to the glory of the Lord himself.”  As a church, it’s critical that we handle the resources that God has entrusted to us in a way that glorifies Him.

 

We want to answer 5 questions in today’s study:

 

  1. When should I give?
    • Chap. 9:1-5
      • Twice it mentions the phrase “be ready.”  How were they to be ready?
      • 1 Cor. 16:2  “on the first day of the week set aside”
        • giving is our responsibility individually and corporately
        • there are two ways you could understand Paul’s meaning
      • Giving in terms of consistency
        • Give as often as you are paid.  (weekly, bi-monthly, or monthly)
      • Giving in terms of frequency
        • Give weekly.  Divide your gift by the number of weeks.  This then forms a good habit.
    • Principle:  Give Regularly.

 

  1. Where should I give?
    • 2 Cor. 9 - two key phrases 1) unprepared (vs. 4),  2) prepare beforehand (vs. 5)
    • 1 Cor. 16:2  - phrase “on the first day of the week”, which we know is Sunday.
      • The early church worshiped corporately on Sunday (Acts 20:7).
      • Rev. 1:10 called Sunday the Lord’s day because Jesus rose from the dead and it has a lot to do with why we worship on Sunday.
      • It was on Sunday that the first Christians were baptized with the Holy Spirit (church was born) on the day of Pentecost.
    • Principle:  Give at church.  Give worshipfully.
      • It is certainly acceptable to give to para-church organizations and to worthy causes.
      • But the first responsibility we have is to give to our home church.  In other words, give where we are receiving \ being fed.
      • Need to see giving as worship.
        • At Calvary we include our offering as part of worship time (music and offering).
      • Giving is something we shouldn’t wait to make decisions about or wait to do (note the phrase in vs. 5 “prepare beforehand”).  Don’t wait until you get to church to make the decision.  Prepare your gift before you come.  

 

  1. How much should I give?
    • 2 Cor. 9:6
      • Tithe means a tenth.  In the Old Testament, there wasn’t only 1 tithe but actually as many as 3 - to the Levites (Duet 14:27), for the sacred feasts (Duet 14:22-26), and in the third year a tithe for the poor (Duet 14:28-29).  So people were giving between  20-30% as tithes.
        • So arguing about whether we are to give a tithe or not isn’t likely based upon a biblical understanding.
      • Are we to tithe?  Paul didn’t command it in this very extensive section on giving in the New Testament.  Nowhere in the NT does is a tithe commanded.
      • It does teach that we are to give proportionately.  This is called grace giving, because we respond to God’s grace in our lives by giving back to Him.
    • 1 Cor. 16:2 – “storing up as he may prosper” – this gives us the idea of giving proportionally.  Giving as you have been given.  It’s not about the amount, but it’s a matter of proportion.  The widow gave it all!
    • We shouldn’t be asking how much we should give.  That is really asking how little we can give.
      • Tithing pre-dates the Law, so tithing isn’t just a part of the OT Law that we should ignore.  Abraham in Gen. 14, Jacob in Gen. 28 both tithed a 10% before the law was given.
      • We shouldn’t just ignore this.  This is a good guideline for us to consider and model.  It’s a good place to start.  Giving back to the Lord that which is His already.
      • Some have used the 10/10/80 Rule that is taking your income and giving 10% to the Lord, putting 10% in savings, and living off the remaining 80%.  This is also a good starting point.
    • Principle:  Give Proportionately.

 

  1. Is there a right and a wrong way to give?
    • 2 Cor. 9:7 – give as he purposes in his heart, not grudgingly, God loves a cheerful giver.  Cheerful means hilarious.  How many thought this morning’s offering was hilarious?
      • Example:  Parent buys and gives children some candy.  Then asks for a piece.  The child looks up and says “Mine!” Or “Take only one.”  You think as a parent, ‘That ungrateful child!’
      • Application:  God is our parent who has provided our every need.  Sadly, we often think just like this child.
      • The problem is that we think of our money as belonging to us and that we earned it ourselves, but that’s not true.  Duet. 8:18 says that God gave us the skills to earn an income.  In fact, He gives us the health to work and promotion in our workplace.
    • Principle:  Give Cheerfully.

 

  1. What happens when I give?
    • I am blessed. 
      • 2 Cor. 9:8 – with God’s grace (key words:  all, always, every, sufficiency, abundance)
      • 2 Cor. 9:9 – with Christian character  (our righteousness will last forever when we give with an open hand; it becomes a test of our faithfulness and obedience)
      • 2 Cor. 9:10 – with monetary multiplication (not a prosperity doctrine, promising 100:1 returns, not get rich quick schemes, or lotto)
        • Luke 6:38 says “give and it will be given to you… with the same measure that you give.”  Not talking about giving to get, but giving in response to God’s grace.
        • God promises to meet our needs, not our greed’s.  He has a way of multiplying our remaining resources when we approach it biblically. 
        • Many of our rewards will be stored away for us in Heaven.  You can’t take it with you, but you can send it ahead, by investing in His Kingdom.
      • 2 Cor. 9:8 – with everything enrichment (enriched in everything for all liberality)
        • God replenishes our supply so we can give more.  You can’t out give God.
    • Principle:  Give Expectantly.
    • Needs are met.
      • 2 Cor. 9:12 – the needs of people inside and outside of our church are met through our giving.
    • God is glorified.
      • 2 Cor. 9:12 – is anyone glorifying God because of my giving?

 

Summary \ Application

It’s easy for people to have the wrong idea when a pastor speaks on this subject, as Allen mentioned in his introduction.  There seems to be a feeling that it is self-seeking.  As pastor of our church, Allen is president of a non-profit organization with an annual budget in excess of $800,000.00.  The expectation is that next year we will hit $1,000,000.00.  It seems like a large amount, but I doubt that many understand what it really costs to do ministry like we do ministry.  The average retail cost per square foot in Austin is between $13-15 per year.  We pay only about half this amount and are blessed.  We have over 35,000 square feet, so you do the math to see what it costs.  Also, this time of year the air heats up and the AC bills for our homes start to reflect it.  For Calvary, our bill can be between $3000-$4000 per month to light and heat or cool our building. It quickly adds up.

 

While making the New 2 Calvary video,  Allen went back to the clubhouse at the apt complex where Calvary Austin had it’s very first bible study.  It was tiny.  From there, they showed the hotel where we met and then the Adventist Church where we rented space.   It’s amazing to think about the growth and how these compare to our current building.  God has done amazing things to grow this ministry and He has always been faithful.

 

If you were to think about the number of adults that attend Calvary Austin and call it their church home and if every one were to give, for an example a 10% of the average per capita income of Austin, our church budget would be around $2,000,000.00 instead of the $800,000.00.  It’s amazing to think about what God wants for our church.  What does He want us to do, to change, to reach our community for Christ?  We don’t hesitate to spend large sums to reach people, such as our Easter service where we spent in excess of $15,000.  But how can you put a price tag on a soul?  How can you put a price on the people who accepted Christ, or the people in our church who stood crying as their loved ones walked forward to receive salvation.  What does God want us to do now (taught in April 2001)? We are halfway through a 6-year lease.  Should we negotiate for an extension, or should we lease more space, or buy land and build?  Where does He want us to be?  Who would He add to our staff?  What kinds of outreaches does He want us to do, that we are not doing now?  How many more people does He want us to help financially get through rough times, both inside and outside of the church?  How many more missionaries does He want us to support?  Those are the things we get excited about and wonder what God has.

 

Just as Allen and Andrea ask God what He wants to do through them for the future of Calvary Austin, we also should be asking God this same question. In Malachi 3, we see that when we don’t give we are robbing God, but there is more there that needs to be emphasized.  Just after this portion, God says “prove me in this.”  You don’t think that God means what He says?  You don’t think your financial condition will improve if you obey?  He says “test me.”  How many have taken God at His Word and tried it?  I think many would tell us that they have and that God is faithful.  He has blessed them.  Will you test God in this and trust Him?

 

In closing, the very last words in the chapter say “thanks be to God for His indescribable gift.”  It reminds us that anything we give pails in comparison to what He gave.   God the Father sent His only son to die on the cross for our sins.  Have you received Him and His payment for your sins?  Ask Him today to forgive you and be your Savior and Lord.
