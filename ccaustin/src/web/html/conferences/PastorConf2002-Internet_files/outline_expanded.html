Define Your Milestones
Identify the steps necessary to get to your goals
Locate dependencies to determine the estimated end date (the long straw)
Make milestones small and manageable, but don’t take faith out of it
Layout your plan on a calendar, or use project management software (Microsoft Project, etc.)
Take measurements often and adjust as necessary

Break Down Each Milestone
Each milestone should be divided into iterations
Each iteration defines a small set of functionality/features to create
Iterations will also include bugs that are high priority
An iteration ends when all features and high priority bugs are completed
Iterations should be scheduled just before they begin, and the plan revisited daily/weekly

Identify Your Risks
Risks are unexpected events that could jeopardize your milestones
Business risks usually involve large shifts in focus, personnel
Technical risks usually involve unexpected costs, integration problems, more complexity to problems than estimated
Identify your risks and list one or more contingencies and/or mitigations

Example Risk Document Entry
2.6 Shortage of participating team members

2.6.1 Risk Magnitude or Ranking
Quite Likely

2.6.2 Description
Too few participating team members to reach originally scheduled deadlines.

2.6.3 Impacts
Some members are overworked and project quality is adversely affected.

2.6.4 Indicators
Artifacts are submitted later than designated deadlines. The quality of the artifacts is compromised at various levels.

2.6.5 Mitigation Strategy
Develop a reasonable schedule to not overwork developers. Use prior past project experience, interests, and current technical knowledge base to assign members to project.

2.6.6 Contingency Plan
Reevaluate the project schedule and discuss with customers the possibility of extending the date of the deliverables.

Site Layout/Design
A site design and layout is a representation of your church
Shy away from animated graphics
Use simple graphics to communicate intent
Visit 10-25 corporate web sites and learn what you like and don’t like

Site Layout/Design (con’t)
Produce a professional site
Consider using other web sites to guide you in your layout and HTML
Learn about Human Computer Interaction (HCI) to understand how users think and look for things
Don’t base essential controls (menus, important text) on things the user may not have installed (Flash)

Intro to HCI
The research found that people:
Processed information based on the hierarchy presented.
Used headlines, photos, and graphic images as entry points.
Processed more headlines than photos.
Typically processed a page from right to left; sometimes in a sideways U-pattern (refuting the old left to right, top to bottom thinking).
Typically did not read "below the fold“ (don’t scroll down)
Did not necessarily respond better to bigger images.
Were particularly attracted to images of people

Intro to HCI (con’t)
How can this help us engage our Web visitors who scan a page for 15 seconds?
If the viewer has trouble determining where to go next, valuable time is wasted and you'll lose the chance to communicate

Intro to HCI (con’t)
Here are some hints:
Assume that people are only going to read the headlines
Make reading accessible in small chunks
Keep copy blocks small
Use small images as visual cues to deliver key messages.
Use color and bold type to drive eyes to the most important information.
Keep your most important messages "above the fold." People may or may not scroll down.
Use photos of people. People are attracted to images of other people

Intro to HCI (con’t)
The bottom line is that people are strapped for time and want information at a glance
You're telling them what's important in your visual hierarchy
And be careful how you string together headlines! It may be the only message people read...

Hosting
Allows your site to be on someone else’s server
Determine server requirements and access
Do you need Java, PHP, ASP, Frontpage support?
What kind of backup services do they offer?
Redundancy?

Co-Location vs. In-House
Co-Location means leasing or providing a server and placing it at a hosting location
Usually cost more $$
Hosting services can include monitoring/management of the server
May also include backup services
Faster access with burst capabilities
In-House is cheaper if broadband is installed already

Broadband/In-House
Useful if hosting the site with your own server at the church
May cost more $$ per month than hosting
Useful for recording and streaming audio/video
Requires monitoring/mgmt of the hardware and connection
What happens if it goes down? Power goes out?
Must manage your own backup plan

Part 5: Protect Your Network and Staff

Protecting Your Network
Why is it important?
Who tries to access our computers and or servers?
What do hackers do once inside the network?

What are Hackers After?
It is all about data and hardware control
Hackers will try from day one to hack into a system with a public internet address
They want to obtain sensitive information
Often, they desire to use it for their own personal gain or prestige
We need to do our best to keep this from taking place

How Do They Do It?
Port Scanners
Denial of Service (DoS)
Exploits (bugs) in software or the operating system
Insecure communications and web applications
Weak servers can open access to the network and interesting servers
For servers to function, some access must be given
Steps have to be taken to close all access except essential services (web, email)

Tools For Security
Firewalls
Intrusion Detection Software
Security Assessment Software
Subscribe to mailing lists for security updates of operating system and applications used
No server is 100% safe, but you can take measures to reduce your risk of intrusion!

Calvary Austin Hacked
Exploits found on our server that had not been updated
Servers were on public network
Firewall software was outdated
Took control of the system, locking us out of the system locally
The only way to regain the server was to unplug it from the wall

Calvary Austin Rebuilds
Server was evaluated for “safe” files and rebuilt
Upgraded the operating system
Upgraded/replaced insecure mail server
Installed better firewall software with tighter security
Turned off services that do not encrypt password and traffic

What You Can Do
Purchase a hardware or software firewall solution
Ensure your servers are on a private network
Determine what services you use (mail server, web server, etc) and check for security upgrades
Turn off all services that are unused or insecure
Co-Locating or hosting your site? Ask what measures they offer and what you may need to buy for your server – it may not be included in the base price!

Securing Your Staff
Searching the internet can bring bad results
We don’t want to restrict access to valuable sites
Content filtering software stops most bad content
It also prevents access during difficult temptations
Forty percent of pastors have visited a porn site; more than a third have done so in the last year. Christianity Today, 2001.
Resolve to protect yourself and your staff!
